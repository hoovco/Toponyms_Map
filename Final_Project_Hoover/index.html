Toponyms and Archaeological sites in Piura, Peru

Use the dropdown menus to access information about toponyms. Use the switches located in "Select Toponyms" to toggle toponym types. Use the drop down located at the top right corner to switch base maps, and use the dropdown located in the bottom left corner to toggle district boundaries and archaeological site visibility.

Toponyms are the reason places have names. In Peru, the Toponyms give a clue as to the history of the area and the people inhabiting it. We can see remnants of langugaes such as Quechua, Chimuan, Muchik, and Sec in many place names.

Toponyms with the types of CHE, CHU and SEC generally are attributed to the Tall치n around coastal Piura.

Toponyms associated with CAN, PON, TE and GARA may possibly be associated with more northern cultures, especially around the Loja, Ecuador area.

Toponyms that are labeled Quechua are associated with the Incan expansion into the area. Terms like Suyo (roughly means state or nation), Bamba/ Pampa is a place derterminer, and other Quechua words such as Pirqa (wall) appear in these toponyms.
